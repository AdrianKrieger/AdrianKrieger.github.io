I. Introduction: Mission Objective & Social Value

The core objective was to design a complete Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellite system, from conceptualization to detailed subsystem budgeting, adhering to strict mission performance and cost constraints. The mission required the satellite to carry a complex optical payload for remote sensing and data collection, positioning it as a key tool for environmental monitoring and predictive analysis.

The social value of such a mission lies in enabling precise, global-scale data acquisition, which is crucial for modern climate research, disaster response planning, and agricultural optimization. The design process focused heavily on minimizing risk while maximizing data return over a projected five-year lifespan.

II. Design & Analysis: Systems Definition

Vehicle Architecture and Subsystems

The conceptual design utilized a nadir-pointing architecture for a Sun-Synchronous Orbit (SSO) at an altitude of 600 km to ensure consistent lighting conditions for the payload. The design centered on a modular bus housing critical subsystems:

  • Propulsion: Defined delta-V requirements for orbit maintenance and end-of-life de-orbit, selecting a low-thrust electric propulsion system for efficiency.
  • Electrical Power System (EPS): Sized solar panels and Li-ion battery arrays based on worst-case power consumption, ensuring a minimum 15% power margin throughout the mission.
  • Structural & Thermal: Modeled primary load-bearing structures and designed a passive thermal control system supplemented by strategic heater placement.

III. Evaluation: Feasibility and Risk Assessment

Mass, Power, and Budget Compliance

A comprehensive margin analysis was performed against established industry standards. The preliminary mass budget achieved a 25% margin against the target launch vehicle capability, and the final power budget retained a 17% margin during the eclipse phase.

The design successfully passed all required system-level checks in the compliance matrix. A critical risk assessment focused primarily on thermal stress during payload operations and long-term degradation of the battery system in orbit, proposing redundancy in key systems.

IV. My Contribution & Role

As a key member of the design team, my primary role was the Mission Analyst and Subsystem Design Lead for structural integrity and electrical power. My contributions included:

  • System Sizing: Performing initial sizing calculations for the solar array geometry and battery capacity based on orbital parameters and payload demands.
  • Structural Design: Developing the preliminary Computer-Aided Design (CAD) model and optimizing the primary load-bearing structure in CATIA.
  • Budgeting: Maintaining the mass, power, and delta-V budgets and ensuring all margins complied with the mission requirements.
  • Report Synthesis: Co-authoring the final technical report, focusing on the feasibility and compliance sections of the overall design.
